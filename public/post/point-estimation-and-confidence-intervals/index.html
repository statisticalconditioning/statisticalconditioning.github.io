Point Estimation and Confidence Intervals

Oct 16, 2017 2 min read

Point Estimates

We want an unbiases and efficient point estimate. A point estimate is unbiased if the center of the sampling distribution centers on the true parameter, or, said differently, if the mean of the point estimate’s sampling distribution is the population mean.

A point estimator is efficient if the standard error (standard deviation of the sampling distribution) is relatively small.

We often use the sample statistic as the point estimate of the population parameter. For example, the sample mean, standard deviation or proportion can be used to estimate the population mean, standard deviation for porportion, respectively. For typical distributions such as the normal distribution, the sample statistic is an unbiased and efficient estimate of the population parameter.

Confidence Intervals (CI)

A confidence interval is an interval generated by a procedure that has a fixed probabilty of containing the parameter over a large number of random samples.

Any specific CI either does, or does not contain the population parameter. We do not know which. In other words, we can’t say that our specific CI contains the population paramter with 95% confidence.

Confidence intervals are constructed similarly to determining the critical value of a statisitcal test. But instead of centering the sampling distribution on the null value, we center it on the point estimate.

The confidence interval indicates about how close the population parameter should be to the point estimates over a large number of samples of the same size sampled at random from the population.

Conceputally the confidence interval is calculated as follows:

\[ \text{Confidene Interval} = \text{Point Estimate} \pm \text{Test statistic} \times \text{SE} \]

The confidence interval describes how the method performs over many, many random samples.

z score

If we know the population variance (or standard deviation) we can calculate the CI as follows: \[ CI_z = \bar{X} \pm z(\sigma_\bar{X}). \] Calculate a 95% confidence interval with a z score as follows:

\[ 95\%CI = \bar{x} \pm 1.96(\sigma_\bar{X}) \]
